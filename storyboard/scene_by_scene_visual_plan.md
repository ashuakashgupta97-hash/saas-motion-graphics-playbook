# Scene-By-Scene Visual Plan

## Scene 1: Title Reveal

- Motion: oval or rail-based title reveal
- Focus: product name and tagline
- Purpose: establish confidence immediately

## Scene 2: Scale Reveal

- Motion: screenshot arrival, floating cards, count callout
- Focus: volume and operating spread
- Purpose: explain why the problem matters

## Scene 3: Decision Constellation

- Motion: scattered UI/doc cards around a center statement, then shift into question rows
- Focus: complexity
- Purpose: make hidden review logic visible

## Scene 4: Existing Workflow Baseline

- Motion: multi-window mockup or anchored screenshot with supporting overlays
- Focus: digital system already exists
- Purpose: show that AI enhances a workflow rather than replacing it

## Scene 5: AI Layer Reveal

- Motion: table pan, highlight band, click target, reasoning modal
- Focus: recommendation columns and explainability
- Purpose: connect AI directly to an existing review surface

## Scene 6: Engine View

- Motion: numbered steps, connected nodes, horizontal path, or compressed multi-agent flow
- Focus: what happens behind the scenes
- Purpose: explain intelligence without becoming too technical

## Scene 7: Proof Scene

- Motion: split-screen between source artifact and extracted/structured output
- Focus: how messy inputs become structured decisions
- Purpose: demonstrate real utility

## Scene 8: Policy Or Route Proof

- Motion: line draw, route comparison, cost recalculation, exception badge
- Focus: optimized or corrected outcome
- Purpose: show AI doing work a reviewer otherwise would

## Scene 9: Judgement Cards

- Motion: vertical cards, each tied to one complex scenario
- Focus: exceptions that need reasoning
- Purpose: emphasize that not all cases are simple rule checks

## Scene 10: Outcome / KPI Close

- Motion: lane outcomes, dashboard settle, metric reveal
- Focus: reduced effort, reduced turnaround, stronger governance
- Purpose: leave the audience with measurable value
